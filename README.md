# PE / M&A Deal Memo & IC Paper Templates

Open-source templates for investment committee (IC) papers and deal memos - structured around real transaction advisory experience at PwC and EY, Mumbai.

These templates reflect the structure and logic used in actual IC presentations across buy-side and sell-side mandates.

---

## What is an IC Paper?

An Investment Committee (IC) paper is the formal document presented to a fund's investment committee before a deal is approved. It must answer five questions with evidence:

1. **Why this business?** - Strategic rationale and market thesis
2. **Why now?** - Timing, competitive dynamics, founder/management context
3. **Is the price fair?** - Valuation approach, comparable transactions, entry multiple
4. **What could go wrong?** - Key risks and mitigants
5. **How do we make money?** - Return pathway (growth, margin expansion, multiple re-rating, exit)

```

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Deal overview: [Company], [Sector], [Transaction type], [Proposed investment size]
• Investment thesis in 3 bullet points
• Key financials: Revenue, EBITDA, EV, Entry multiple (EV/EBITDA or EV/Revenue)
• Recommendation: APPROVE / APPROVE WITH CONDITIONS / DECLINE

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 2: COMPANY OVERVIEW
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Business description: What does the company do? Who are its customers?
• History and founding story
• Products / services and revenue streams
• Geographies of operation
• Management team: founders, key hires, skin in the game

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 3: MARKET ANALYSIS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Total Addressable Market (TAM): Size, growth rate, source
• Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): The segment this company can realistically capture
• Market structure: fragmented vs. consolidated; organised vs. unorganised
• Key tailwinds driving growth
• Competitive landscape: direct and indirect competitors
• Company's right to win: differentiation, moat, defensibility

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 4: FINANCIAL ANALYSIS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Historical financials (3 years): Revenue, Gross Profit, EBITDA, PAT
• Quality of Earnings summary: Adjusted EBITDA and key normalisations
• Working capital profile: Days receivable, days payable, inventory days
• Net debt / cash position
• Key financial ratios: Gross margin %, EBITDA margin %, Revenue growth %
• Revenue quality: customer concentration, channel mix, repeat vs. new

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 5: VALUATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Proposed Enterprise Value and Equity Value
• Valuation methodology: DCF / Comparable Companies / Precedent Transactions
• Comparable company trading multiples
• Precedent transaction multiples
• Implied entry multiple and benchmarking vs. peers
• Sensitivity analysis: bear / base / bull case

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 6: KEY RISKS & MITIGANTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
| Risk | Severity | Mitigant |
|------|----------|----------|
| Customer concentration | High | Contractual lock-ins, diversification plan |
| Key man dependency | Medium | ESOP structure, succession plan |
| Regulatory risk | Low-Medium | Legal due diligence findings |
| Integration risk | Medium | 100-day plan, management retention |

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 7: RETURN ANALYSIS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Hold period assumption: [X] years
• Exit route: Strategic sale / IPO / Secondary PE
• Exit multiple assumption and rationale
• Projected MOIC and IRR in base case
• Sensitivity: MOIC at different exit multiples and hold periods

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 8: DEAL TERMS & STRUCTURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Proposed investment structure: Primary / Secondary / Mix
• Key SPA terms: Representations & warranties, indemnities, escrow
• Working capital peg and adjustment mechanism
• Conditions precedent to closing
• Post-close covenants and governance rights

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 9: RECOMMENDATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Summary recommendation
• Conditions / open items to be resolved before closing
• Next steps and timeline
```

## Tips for Writing a Strong IC Paper

- **Lead with the insight, not the description.** The IC already knows what the company does. Open with why *this* deal at *this* price makes sense.
- **Show the normalisations clearly.** Every adjustment to EBITDA must be named, sized, and justified. Vague "one-time items" get challenged.
- **Stress-test your own thesis.** The best IC papers explicitly argue against themselves, then explain why the upside outweighs the risk.
- **Know your multiples.** Entry price is everything in PE. Know comparable transaction multiples cold before you walk into the room.
